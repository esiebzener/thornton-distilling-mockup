The Archive
The Archive

It was a brewery before it was a story.
A vault before it was a still.

Built into limestone in 1857. Locked up by Prohibition. Raided by the FBI. Restored, board by board, in 2019. Today the same building pours spirits drawn from the same artesian well that fed its first brew kettle.

1857

Built

A limestone brewery rises beside the spring‑fed Thorn Creek. John Bielfeldt taps the artesian well; the bricks come from the bluff that named the town.

1920

Seized
Seized

Volstead Act. The brewery closes by federal order. The Capone organization moves in. The limestone vault — built to age lagers — becomes a hideout.

1944

Raided
Raided

An illegal still discovered in the cellar. FBI agents seal the building. For seven decades it stands shuttered — a monument to the trade it was forced to hide.

2019

Reborn

Two founders buy the building. The same well is re‑tapped. The first legal spirit drawn off the same stone in a century goes into a copper still on the original floor.
